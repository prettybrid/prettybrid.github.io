About the Artist

Prettybrid

Prettybrid is a visual artist whose practice spans three decades, beginning in the 1990s when she left the darkroom for Photoshop — not to abandon photography, but to solve problems the darkroom could not. The seamless composite, the perfect shadow, the image that revealed what the photograph could only imply. She was pioneering digital image-making before it had a name.

The original Altered States of Reality series — fourteen photographs constructed in Photoshop over the course of her life — tells a complete human story. From the body to the sublime. From constraint to dissolution. From the first question a child asks about the world to the moment the self finally releases into landscape.

The Bitcoin Ordinals collection extends this work into a new frontier. Each original photograph is brought into AI — not to replace the artist’s vision, but to reveal what was always hidden inside it. Prettybrid directs, refines, and curates every piece. The AI is a tool, not an author.

All works are inscribed as Bitcoin Ordinals — permanently and immutably recorded on the most secure digital ledger in existence. To own a Prettybrid Ordinal is to own a chapter of a life’s work, written permanently into Bitcoin.

Artist Statement

“I began this work in the 1990s trying to solve something the darkroom couldn’t solve. I wanted lines that disappeared. Shadows that obeyed. Worlds that could exist inside other worlds without the seams showing. Photoshop gave me that — and something I didn’t expect. It gave me a way to make visible the psychological landscapes I had been trying to create in the darkroom all along.

The series has a narrative order because it follows the arc of a life. Not my life specifically — the life. Truth comes first because the body knows before the mind does. Feel comes last because that is what remains when everything else has been shed. Between those two poles is the whole of human experience: the structures built over us, the games we are asked to play, the questions we never consented to, the moment the curtain finally lifts.

When I bring these images into AI, I am not starting over. I am asking the work what it always wanted to become. Sometimes the AI finds something I knew was there but couldn’t reach. Sometimes it finds something I didn’t know at all. Both are true to the practice.

I inscribe this work on Bitcoin because it deserves to be permanent. Not stored on a server that can be deleted. Not hosted by a company that can go bankrupt. Written into the blockchain itself — immutable, decentralized, existing as long as Bitcoin exists. That is the only archive worthy of a life’s work.”

— Prettybrid, 2026
